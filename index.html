University of Maine Field Hockey

Welcome to the UMaine Field Hockey website!
This page is for the UMaine Women's Field Hockey Black Bears!
Our team consists of 19 female athletes from all around the world.
Each player brings their own set of skills and abilities to the team and are lead by two highly qualified coaches.
The fall season begins in August with preseason and carries out all the way to November.
This season we have 5 new freshman joining the team. Three of which are local and the other two are from Canada.
Currently our team is 12 games into season, out of which 3 are conference games.
Overall our girls are 8-4 for wins and are 2-1 for wins in conference play.

Coaches






The University of Maine Field hockey team is coached by Josette Babineau, who is assisted by Michelle Simpson
Josette Babineau enters her 10th season as head coach of the Black Bears
Michelle Simpson enters her 1st season as assistant coach of the Black Bears
Babineau graduated in 1993 from the University of New Brunswick with a degree in business administration
Last year (2016) Simpson was inducted into the University of Albany Hall of Fame for her amazing career accomplishments with the Great Danes field hockey program.



Awards

Currently the University of Maine is in second place in conference play.

UMaine is also currently ranked number 31 in the NCAA D1 Field Hockey rankings.

On September 6th 2017, Libby Riedl was awarded Offensive player of the week by the American East Conference.

On October 3rd 2017, Brianna Ricker was awarded Offensive player of the week by the American East Conference.

On October 10th 2017, Madison Cummings was awarded Offensive player of the week by the American East Conference.
